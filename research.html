Spatiotemporal Physics-Informed Machine Learning Model for Weather Prediction in Uganda

Introduction

As Uganda faces the realities of climate change, accurate weather prediction becomes critical. My research focuses on enhancing forecast accuracy by integrating machine learning with physics-informed principles, especially in data-scarce environments.

Research Motivation

Accurate and reliable weather prediction is vital for Uganda, where agriculture, water resources, disaster management, and transportation heavily depend on timely and precise forecasts. However, traditional forecasting methods often face challenges like high computational demands, sensitivity to initial conditions, and limited accuracy, especially in data-scarce regions.

Motivated by these challenges, my research focuses on developing a hybrid spatiotemporal physics-informed machine learning (PINN) model for weather forecasting. By combining deep learning architectures (CNNs, LSTMs, GNNs, FNOs) with physics-based constraints like the Navier-Stokes equations, energy conservation, and moisture balance, this research aims to improve prediction accuracy, generalization, and interpretability.

Ultimately, my goal is to enhance early warning systems, support sustainable agriculture, and strengthen climate resilience in Uganda and other data-scarce regions.
